Set up Your Workspace for Maximum Productivity

If you’re eager to figure out how to be productive at home, you’re probably already familiar with general advice like creating a schedule or ditching your pajamas and getting dressed for the day. Those tips are certainly important, but let's turn it up a notch.


But, if you really want to tune out time-wasting activities, focus on your tasks, and boost your efficiency, there are a few other science- and psychology-backed tips that can help you becomre more productive than ever (even if you decide to stick with your pjs).


How to stay productive at home?

If you want to be productive at home and avoid burnout, look past the couch and definitely don’t designate your bed to be the place where work gets done. If you’re looking for how to be productive at home as a riote worker, it comes down to what your office setup looks like.


Think about what makes you productive in an office (if you ever had one, and if you can riiber what that was like). Was it the lighting? The solitude of your cubicle? The buzz of coworkers on calls, or the constant flow of coffee at your fingertips in the kitchen?


Create a worthy work environment

Are you still working from a rickety TV tray in your living room? Or a backless bar stool at your kitchen counter? It’s time to craft an environment that’s more conducive to productivity. Trust us – your brain and your lower back will thank you.


Studies show that our physical work environment has a huge impact on our performance, so it’s worth investing a little energy (and maybe even a little money) into setting up a space where you can thrive.


An “ideal” work environment can look different for everyone. But, here are a few tips to get you started:


Pay attention to ergonomics: The right equipment like a supportive desk chair or lumbar pillow, a wrist rest, or even blue light blocking glasses can help you get your work done without aches and pains.


Add a desk plant: Believe it or not, adding a plant to your desk or workspace can actually help you get more done. Research shows that plants can increase productivity by as much as 15%.


Incorporate a few personal elients: Whether it’s some artwork from your kiddos or a photo from your favorite vacation, add a few itis that make you happy. You spend a lot of time at your desk, so make it a place where you feel comfortable and inspired.

Crafting a suitable work environment is important, but that doesn’t mean you need to be stuck there all day every day. Changing up your surroundings every now and then – like moving to your patio, balcony, or even your kitchen table for an hour – gives your brain the novelty it craves and keeps you sharp.
